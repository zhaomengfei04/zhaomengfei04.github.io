Research Interests

Reinforcement Learning | Neural Network Pruning | Continual Leanrning | Optimization Theory | Evolutionary Computation

My current research focuses on combining optimization and pruning techniques to find the most minimal yet effective models in Reinforcement Learning. I am particularly interested in optimization processes, including policy gradient optimization and non-gradient optimization based on Evolutionary Strategy (ES). My work has led me to realize that overparameterization is not necessarily about needing so many parameters for neural networks to express themselves; instead, it is a strategy that helps with the optimization process. Additionally, I am very interested in continual learning, and I am exploring how these areas can be integrated to improve RL systems.
